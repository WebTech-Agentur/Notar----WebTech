General Terms and Conditions

1. Scope of Application

The following terms apply to the use of the website provided by the NotarWeb Notary Office. The relationship between the notary and the client is primarily governed by mandatory statutory regulations (e.g., the Federal Notary Act and the Notarization Act), which take precedence over these terms.

2. Notarial Services and Fees

Notaries hold a public office and act independently and impartially. They are not representatives of a single party, but independent legal advisors for all parties involved.

The fees for notarial services are strictly regulated by the Court and Notary Costs Act (Gerichts- und Notarkostengesetz - GNotKG). Fee agreements deviating from these statutory regulations are legally prohibited.

3. Use of the Website

The content of this website has been created with the greatest possible care. However, we do not guarantee the accuracy, completeness, or timeliness of the information provided.

The information provided on this website does not constitute binding legal advice and cannot replace a personal consultation with a notary.

4. Liability

The notary's liability for official acts is governed by § 19 of the Federal Notary Act (BNotO). Regarding this website, liability for slightly negligent breaches of duty is excluded, provided these do not relate to essential contractual obligations, damages resulting from injury to life, body, or health, or guarantees.

5. Appointment Bookings

Appointments scheduled via our website (e.g., through Calendly) are non-binding consultation requests until confirmed by our office. The preparation of complex drafts prior to an appointment may trigger statutory fees even if the notarization subsequently does not take place.

6. Applicable Law

The law of the Federal Republic of Germany applies exclusively.
