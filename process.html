Process & Fees

Transparency is the cornerstone of trust. We explain exactly how our collaboration works and how the statutory fees are calculated.

Your Path to Legal Certainty

Every notarial act follows a structured process to ensure that all parties are fully informed and their interests are protected.

1

Initial Contact & Data Collection

You reach out to us via phone, email, or our online booking system. We will briefly discuss your request and ask for the necessary key information and documents required to prepare the draft.

2

Draft Preparation

Based on the information provided, we prepare a legally sound draft of the required document. This draft will be sent to all involved parties (usually by email or postal mail) with sufficient time for review prior to the appointment.

3

Review & Consultation

During the review period, you have the opportunity to ask questions or request changes. If necessary, we can arrange a comprehensive preliminary consultation to clarify complex legal consequences.

4

The Notarial Recording (Beurkundung)

At the appointment in our office, the notary reads the entire document aloud to all parties. This is a vital step to ensure understanding, answer final questions, and make any last-minute adjustments before the document is signed and sealed.

5

Execution (Abwicklung)

After signing, our office takes care of the execution. Depending on the transaction, this includes communicating with authorities, the land registry, the commercial register, and the tax office until the matter is fully completed.

Transparent & Statutory Fees

Notaries do not negotiate individual hourly rates. Notary fees in Germany are strictly regulated by law to ensure absolute neutrality and equal access to justice.

The Court and Notary Costs Act (GNotKG)

All notarial fees are calculated exclusively based on the Court and Notary Costs Act (Gerichts- und Notarkostengesetz - GNotKG). The fees are not determined by the amount of time spent on your case, but by the so-called "business value" (Geschäftswert) of the transaction.

Examples of Business Value: For a property purchase, it is the purchase price. For a GmbH formation, it is the share capital. For an inheritance contract, it is the net value of the estate.

Inclusive Services: The statutory fee for a notarization (Beurkundung) generally includes the comprehensive preliminary consultation, draft preparation, the actual recording appointment, and the provision of standard copies.

Execution Costs: Additional, smaller fees apply for our activities in executing the document (Vollzugsgebühren) and for expenditures (telephone, postage).


We understand that costs are a concern. We are happy to provide you with an estimated calculation of the expected notary and court fees prior to your appointment based on the anticipated business value.
