Privacy Policy

1. An overview of data protection

General remarks

The following information will provide you with an easy to navigate overview of what will happen with your personal data when you visit this website. The term "personal data" comprises all data that can be used to personally identify you.

Notaries are subject to strict professional secrecy obligations. Your data is handled with the utmost confidentiality.

How do we record your data?

We collect your data when you share it with us. This may, for instance, be information you enter into our contact form.

Other data is recorded by our IT systems automatically or after you consent to its recording during your website visit. This data comprises primarily technical information (e.g., web browser, operating system, or time the site was accessed).

What are the purposes we use your data for?

A portion of the information is generated to guarantee the error-free provision of the website. Other data may be used to analyze your user patterns. Furthermore, data provided via the contact form is used exclusively for the purpose of communicating with you regarding your notarial inquiry.

3. Use of Calendly for Appointment Bookings

We use the services of Calendly LLC to manage online appointment bookings. When you book an appointment, the data you enter (such as name, email, phone number) is transferred to Calendly.

Calendly's privacy policy can be viewed at: https://calendly.com/privacy.

The legal basis for processing this data is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR, as the appointment booking is a pre-contractual step towards utilizing our notarial services.

4. Your rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR)

To exercise these rights, simply contact us at the address provided above.
